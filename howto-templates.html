Booking Blood Labs

If patient is asking about blood labs, send following information:
For our blood labs we have tons of different types - the way it would work consists of the following:

Step 1: We would send our BioSync paperwork over to you (this basically will tell us what you are interested in knowing, what you want to get tested, certain problems that may be happening etc)

Step 2: The doc will then look over everything once submitted, and see which areas are most important/prominent to be testing

Step 3: Once the paperwork has been reviewed - we will have you come in and get a consultation with our Nurse (and Silvio), to make sure everyone is on the same page with what is being tested, and to go over any questions you may have

Step 4: We can then get you set up with the nurse (normally same day as consultation) so she can draw your blood

Step 5: Once we send out the test, and get the results back, we will have you come in for a follow up appt to go over your results and findings

Step 6: After we get all of that out of the way, you can either choose to stop here or continue on to know more about our total transformation program, which will allow you to receive a full treatment plan, dietary restrictions and lists, recommended services/supplements, workout schedules and so much more!!
Send BioSync email to their email address

Alert Silvio that someone will be doing the blood lab, and that paperwork was sent out
Once paperwork has been submitted - notify Silvio so he can start the blood lab process
Get patient booked for a consultation + take payment for consultation
Try to book patient on a day that the Nurse is in, so they can get the consultation and lab on the same day (CONFIRM WITH NURSE BEFORE BOOKING)

How to put a patient into WebPT

Make sure patient filled out the paperwork from Salesforce
If the ID and insurance card were not uploaded, make sure to ask them for it and take a copy (keep for later)
Open WebPT from the favorited bookmarks
It should automatically let you login under PeytonSamons
If it gives you an error saying someone is already logged in, click "yes oust them"
Once the homepage loads in, look to the left and find where it says "patient manager"
Click add patient
In the "Patient Info" section:
Fill out the top with their prefix (Mr or Mrs), first and last name, birthday, and gender
In the "Addresses" section (underneath Patient Info):
Hit add address (it should be underlined)
Fill out address provided by the patient
In the Insurance section:
Add the insurance provided by the patient
Verify if insurance is through their job (PPO) or just regular health insurance
Add the subscriber (member) ID and the group number (should be provided on the insurance card)
Verify who the guarantor is - if they are not the guarantor, MAKE SURE TO GET THE GUARANTOR'S INFORMATION TO ADD INTO THE FILE (birthday, address, phone number)
In the Case section:
Click add case
Fill out each section that says REQUIRED
Title the case the abbreviation for whichever insurance they have and the current FULL date
BCBS - blue cross blue shield, UHC - united health care etc.. (XX/XX/XXXX)
If they have two insurances - find out which is primary and which is secondary
Should say "supplementary" on back of card for secondary - If still not sure - ask Silvio
Click the drop down arrow next to PRIMARY INSURANCE, the insurance you added should be the only option (if they have a secondary insurance, click the drop down arrow next to secondary and add it there)
Related Cause:
The related cause is always "None of the above" unless they are an Auto accident WITH A CLAIM
Make sure to specify if they were at fault of the accident or not

The doctor who referred them should be in the intake forms, if not ask the patient
