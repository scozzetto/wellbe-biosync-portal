What is Physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession focused on restoring and maintaining physical function, mobility, and strength. Our licensed physiotherapists use evidence-based techniques including manual therapy, therapeutic exercises, movement education, and advanced modalities to help you recover from injury, manage chronic conditions, and optimize your physical performance.

Benefits of Physiotherapy

Restored Mobility

Regain full range of motion and functional movement patterns

Strength Building

Rebuild muscle strength and endurance through targeted exercises

Balance & Coordination

Improve stability and reduce fall risk through specialized training

Movement Education

Learn proper body mechanics to prevent future injuries

Manual Therapy

Hands-on techniques to improve joint mobility and soft tissue health

Performance Enhancement

Optimize movement efficiency for sports and daily activities

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a referral for physiotherapy?

In most cases, you don't need a referral to see a physiotherapist. However, some insurance plans may require a physician's referral for coverage. We recommend checking with your insurance provider. Our team can help you understand your coverage options and work with your healthcare providers as needed.

What should I expect during my first visit?

Your first visit includes a comprehensive assessment of your condition, medical history, movement patterns, strength, and flexibility. Your physiotherapist will discuss your goals and develop a personalized treatment plan. The session may include some initial treatment techniques and you'll receive education about your condition and home exercises.

How long will my treatment take?

Treatment duration varies based on your condition, goals, and response to therapy. Acute injuries may resolve in 4-6 weeks, while chronic conditions or post-surgical rehabilitation may take several months. Your physiotherapist will provide realistic expectations and adjust your treatment plan based on your progress.
